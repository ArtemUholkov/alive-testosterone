Testosterone Replacement Therapy (TRT) is a treatment that involves supplementing the body with testosterone when natural production decreases. It's commonly used to treat hypogonadism in men, a condition where the body doesn’t produce enough testosterone.

Men who exhibit symptoms of low testosterone, such as decreased libido, fatigue, erectile dysfunction, loss of muscle mass, and mood changes, may be candidates for TRT. Diagnosis is typically confirmed through blood tests measuring testosterone levels.

TRT can be administered through injections and creams. The choice of method often depends on patient preference, lifestyle, and medical considerations.

Possible side effects may include acne, fluid retention, increased red blood cell count, and breast enlargement. Long-term risks might involve worsening sleep apnea.

Maintaining a healthy lifestyle is crucial during TRT. Regular exercise, a balanced diet, adequate sleep, and managing stress levels can optimize the benefits of treatment.
